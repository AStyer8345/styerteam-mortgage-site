Mortgage Rates Rallied Today. I Still Can't Tell You Why Tomorrow Matters.

Here's the thing about being a loan officer in March 2026: mortgage rates have been a bloodbath for weeks. Every morning I'd wake up expecting them to bleed lower. And this morning, they finally moved higher. But not for the reason you'd think.

Let me be straight with you. I've been doing this long enough to know when I'm chasing my tail. And today is one of those days.

What Actually Happened This Morning

Bonds rallied. MBS (the bonds that directly impact your mortgage rate) gained 3/8ths of a point. The 10-year yield dropped 7 basis points and is now sitting just under 4.37%. That's a meaningful move. That's a "hey, maybe this is a turning point" kind of move.

Except I don't know if it is.

Here's why: three separate things pushed bonds higher simultaneously, and none of them have anything to do with whether rates are going up or down long-term.

  • Fed Futures Corrected. On Friday morning, investors started adjusting their bets on what the Fed will do. That correction continued into this morning. Bonds like certainty. Even a small shift in Fed expectations can push money into bonds.
  • Pre-Weekend Positioning Unwound. Last week we talked about how it makes sense for bonds to be defensive heading into a weekend when geopolitical risk is high (Iran, oil prices, Middle East tension). Well, Monday rolled around with no major escalation. So traders who had been defensive over the weekend decided to cover those positions. That creates its own little rally.
  • Month-End Momentum. It's March 30th. Month-end positioning creates its own energy in the market. Sometimes bonds catch a bid just because calendar mechanics push money around.

Do you see the pattern? None of those three reasons is about whether the economy is getting stronger or weaker, whether inflation is really solved, or whether oil is going to stay expensive forever.

Why This Is Exhausting to Watch

I say this without exaggeration: it is impossible to predict what happens next.

A loan officer's job is to give you honest answers. And the honest answer right now is that rates move on a thousand little things that have nothing to do with each other. Fed positioning. Geopolitical hedging. Calendar effects. Oil prices. War escalation. Bank balance sheets. International capital flows.

You can't track all of it. I can't track all of it. And nobody on CNBC can either, no matter what they tell you.

This morning looked like a "turning point." Tomorrow morning could look like the opposite. And the day after that could be something else entirely.

That's the reality of March 2026 mortgage rates. It's not boring. It's not predictable. It's exhausting.

What You Actually Need to Know

If you've been thinking about locking in a rate, here's what I'd tell you:

  • Don't wait for perfection. You will not catch the bottom. I won't either. Nobody will.
  • Don't panic on volatility. A nice rally on a Monday (like today) is not evidence of a broader reversal. It's three factors colliding.
  • Lock when your rate meets your comfort level. Not when you think the market is perfect. When you can sleep at night.

That's the only move that actually works.

The market is going to do what it's going to do. I'm going to wake up tomorrow and check it all again. You don't need to live on that roller coaster with me.

Personal Corner

I want to say something that might sound unprofessional coming from a mortgage guy: I genuinely respect people who are sitting on the sidelines right now, frustrated, asking me when it's safe to jump in.

That frustration is legitimate. Rates have been a mess. The geopolitical backdrop is real. Oil is expensive. Nobody knows what the Fed is actually going to do next.

But here's what I've learned: the people who do the best aren't the ones who wait for certainty. Certainty never comes. The people who do well are the ones who make a decision based on their situation (not the market), lock in when it feels right, and then move on with their life.

That's boring. That's not fun to talk about on a Monday morning when bonds are rallying. But it works.

If you've been thinking about your situation—whether you're buying, refinancing, or just trying to understand what's happening—let's talk. I'll tell you what I actually know and what I don't. And then you can make a decision that lets you sleep at night.

That's all any of us can really do.
