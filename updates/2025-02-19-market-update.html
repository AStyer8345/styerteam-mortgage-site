Austin Market at a Glance

The Austin-Round Rock metro area continues its shift toward balance. Here are the key numbers:

  • Median home price: $450,000 (down 2.1% from last month, flat year-over-year)
  • Active listings: Up 18% compared to this time last year
  • Days on market: 62 days average (up from 45 days a year ago)
  • Months of inventory: 3.8 months (approaching a balanced market)

What does this mean? Buyers have more negotiating power than they've had in years. Sellers are more open to negotiating on price, closing costs, and repairs. If you're selling, pricing correctly from day one matters more than ever.

3 Strategies to Win in This Market

  1. Get pre-approved before you start looking. The best deals still go fast. A pre-approval letter means you can move quickly when the right home hits. Get pre-qualified here or apply for full pre-approval.
  2. Don't wait for rates to drop to buy. If rates drop significantly, every sidelined buyer jumps back in, prices go up, and competition increases. Buy when you can afford to and refinance later. Marry the house, date the rate.
  3. Ask about seller concessions. Many sellers will contribute toward closing costs or buy down your rate. A 2-1 buydown can save you thousands in the first two years. Explore loan programs to learn more.

Quick Tip: Understanding Closing Costs

In Texas, expect to pay between 2% and 4% of the purchase price in closing costs. On a $400,000 home, that's roughly $8,000 to $16,000. But closing costs are negotiable — you can ask the seller to cover some or all of them, or ask your lender about credits for a slightly higher rate in exchange for lower upfront costs.

What I'm Watching

  • Fed meeting minutes: Clues about the pace of future rate cuts. The market is pricing in 2-3 cuts in 2025.
  • Existing home sales data: Expected to show a slight uptick as spring kicks off.
  • Austin new construction: New communities opening in Leander, Liberty Hill, and Buda with builder incentives worth watching.
