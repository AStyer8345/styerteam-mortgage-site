Why Should Manor Buyers Use an Independent Mortgage Broker?

Manor is the most affordable entry point into the Austin metro — and that's why it's one of the fastest-growing communities in Travis County. Tesla's Gigafactory sits about 15 minutes south on SH-130, and Samsung's $17B fab is roughly 20 minutes north in Taylor. Manor has gone from a farming town to a booming suburb with big new-construction inventory and improving commercial infrastructure — but also a market where builder lenders push their own incentive packages instead of shopping your loan.

Median sale prices in Manor were $355K as of November 2025, up 6.6% year over year (Redfin). At that price, FHA's $524,225 Travis County limit and TSAHC's income caps both clear easily — meaning more programs are actually open to Manor buyers than to buyers shopping Cedar Park or Westlake. The trade-off is that Manor's combined property tax burden runs higher (roughly 2.27% effective per Ownwell) because the MUD and PID overlays in newer master-planned communities stack on top of city + county + school rates.

Most of Manor's residential growth sits in the 78653 zip. Four communities drive the bulk of new-construction volume:

  • ShadowGlen (78653) — Master-planned with an 18-hole golf course, 4-acre water park, and resort-style pool. Active builders include Terrata Homes, Perry Homes, Meritage, and LGI. Newer Perry/Terrata sections list $499K–$555K (top of Manor's range).

Manor Schools

Manor sits inside Manor Independent School District (MISD), not Pflugerville or Del Valle ISD. Be honest about ratings before you fall in love with a house: Manor High School is rated 2/10 on GreatSchools (below the Texas average for comparable schools). Manor New Tech High and individual elementaries score better in spots — pull the GreatSchools page for a specific address before you write an offer. For families prioritizing higher-rated schools, the Pflugerville ISD line just west of Manor or Round Rock ISD farther north are common alternatives, though prices step up $50K-$100K.

Closing Cost Example: $355K Manor Home

For a $355,000 Manor purchase with 5% down on a conventional loan, expect closing costs in the $7,500–$9,500 range — title insurance and escrow ($1,800–$2,400), lender fees ($1,200–$1,800), recording and survey ($300–$500), prepaid homeowner's insurance and interest ($1,200–$1,800), and 3–4 months of property tax escrow ($2,000–$2,700) at Manor's ~2.27% effective rate. FHA buyers using TSAHC or TDHCA DPA can typically cover most of this from grant funds, which is why Manor remains one of the few Austin-metro markets where sub-$5,000-out-of-pocket closings still happen.

What Loan Programs Are Available for Manor TX Buyers?

FHA Loans

The most popular loan type in Manor. 3.5% down with a 580+ credit score, higher DTI allowances, and flexible guidelines make FHA the go-to for first-time buyers and those rebuilding credit. Manor's price points keep monthly payments manageable even with FHA mortgage insurance.

Conventional Loans

As little as 3% down for qualified buyers. Competitive rates and no upfront mortgage insurance premium. With Manor's median prices well below the $832,750 conforming limit, conventional financing works well for buyers with solid credit and stable income.

VA Loans

Zero down payment, no PMI, and the lowest rates available for eligible veterans and active duty military. With Manor's affordable price points, VA financing makes homeownership achievable with minimal out-of-pocket costs.

TSAHC + TDHCA Down Payment Assistance

Manor's $355K median price sits well under TSAHC's purchase-price ceiling, and Travis County income limits clear cleanly for most working households. TSAHC grants and forgivable seconds plus TDHCA's My First Texas Home can wipe out most of your down payment and closing costs combined — the path to a sub-$5,000-out-of-pocket Manor close.
