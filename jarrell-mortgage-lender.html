Loan Options for Jarrell Home Buyers

Jarrell's price range opens up programs that many Austin-area buyers don't qualify for. Here's what fits.

FHA Loans

3.5% down with credit scores as low as 580. The most popular option for first-time Jarrell buyers. At a $320,000 purchase price, your down payment is roughly $11,200 before closing costs. FHA pairs well with DPA programs to reduce cash needed at closing.

Conventional Loans

As low as 3% down with 620+ credit. No upfront mortgage insurance, and PMI drops off at 80% LTV. Strong option for buyers with solid credit who want to minimize long-term costs. Most Jarrell homes fall well under the $832,750 conforming limit.

USDA Loans

Zero down payment for eligible Jarrell addresses and income levels. Lower mortgage insurance costs than FHA. Parts of Jarrell likely qualify — I verify every address against current USDA maps because this program saves buyers thousands when it applies.

VA Loans

Zero down for eligible veterans and active-duty service members. No monthly PMI. Competitive rates. If you have VA eligibility, this is almost always the best option regardless of what you have saved for a down payment.

Jarrell TX Mortgage FAQ

Jarrell home prices typically range from $270,000 to $420,000, making it one of the most affordable communities in Williamson County. Master-planned neighborhoods like Sonterra, Carmel Creek, and Bryson offer new construction from national builders at price points well below Georgetown or Round Rock. For buyers who want Williamson County without the premium price tag, Jarrell delivers serious value.

Parts of Jarrell likely qualify for USDA Rural Development loans, which offer zero down payment financing for income-eligible buyers. USDA eligibility is address-specific and changes as communities grow. I check every Jarrell buyer's address against current USDA maps because when it applies, it is one of the best loan programs available — no down payment and lower mortgage insurance than FHA.

New construction in Jarrell follows the same loan process as resale, with some key differences. You can lock a rate with a builder float-down option, and the appraisal is based on plans and specs rather than an existing home. Builder lenders often offer incentives like closing cost credits to use their preferred lender, but those rates are not always the best deal once you compare apples to apples. I run a side-by-side comparison so you can see the real numbers before committing.

Jarrell offers significantly lower entry prices than Georgetown — often $50,000 to $100,000 less for comparable square footage and new construction. Both are in Williamson County, but Jarrell is served by Jarrell ISD rather than Georgetown ISD. Jarrell is about 10 minutes north of Georgetown on I-35 and is growing rapidly as buyers seek affordability without leaving the corridor. If your budget is tight but you want new construction in Williamson County, Jarrell deserves a hard look.

Jarrell buyers have access to TSAHC (Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation) and TDHCA (Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs) down payment assistance programs. These can provide 3-5% toward your down payment or closing costs as a grant or forgivable loan. Combined with FHA financing at 3.5% down, many first-time Jarrell buyers get into a home with very little cash out of pocket. I run the eligibility check as part of every pre-approval.
