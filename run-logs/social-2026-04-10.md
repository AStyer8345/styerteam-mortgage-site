## LinkedIn (`afd4a9cc-70ce-4957-9aba-b30694b4a293`)

Most buyers hear FHA = easy approval. That's only half the story.

FHA does have more flexibility on credit scores and down payment than conventional. But there's a cost that doesn't get explained clearly enough at the front end: mortgage insurance that doesn't go away.

With conventional, PMI cancels automatically once you hit 20% equity. With FHA, if you put less than 10% down, the mortgage insurance stays for the life of the loan. The only way out is to refinance.

On a $400,000 loan, that's about $183/month in MIP — every month, until you refinance or sell.

For some buyers, FHA is still the right call. Credit in the 580–640 range, less than 5% saved, recent credit events — FHA has real advantages in those situations.

But I've had buyers come to me already locked into an FHA loan they should have been in conventional from the start. Usually because no one walked them through the full cost comparison.
