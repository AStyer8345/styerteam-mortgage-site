## LinkedIn (text_only, education pillar)

Most refinance pitches skip the only math that actually matters.

Break-even point. Closing costs divided by monthly savings. That number — in months — is how long it takes to pay the refinance off before you see real savings.

If the break-even is 30 months and you sell in 18, you lost money. Doesn't matter how good the rate looked.

I had a client last year save $380/month on paper. Sounds great. Closing costs were $9,500. Break-even was 25 months. They listed the house 14 months later for a job in Dallas. That refinance cost them real money.

The old rule of thumb (1% drop = refinance) ignores your loan balance and how long you plan to stay. A 0.5% drop on a $600K loan beats a 1% drop on a $200K loan.

Three things have to line up before I recommend a refi:

1) Rate drop of at least 0.5–0.75%
2) Break-even shorter than your honest tenure
3) Closing costs under 3% of loan amount

If one of those is off, waiting usually wins.
