Most buyers think mortgage approval is about them.

Credit score. Income. Down payment. Those they can control.

What they don't realize: when you're buying a condo, the building has to qualify too.

Every condo purchase requires the HOA to complete a "condo questionnaire" — a document lenders use to determine if the project is warrantable. Owner-occupancy ratio, reserve fund balance, pending litigation, delinquency rates on dues.

If the HOA fails that review — or refuses to complete it — the deal can fall apart even if the buyer is fully qualified and the unit itself is perfect.
