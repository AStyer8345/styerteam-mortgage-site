Hey *|FNAME|*,

Quick question — did you know the seller can pay for your closing costs?

It's called a seller concession, and it's one of the most underused tools in homebuying. Here's the short version:

When you make an offer, you can negotiate for the seller to contribute a percentage of the purchase price toward your closing costs and prepaids. On a $350,000 home, that can be $10,500 to $21,000 — often enough to cover every dollar you'd owe at closing.

But here's where it gets really interesting.

If there's money left over after covering your costs, those concession dollars can buy down your interest rate. Two ways to do it:

1. A 2-1 buydown — your rate drops 2% in year one, 1% in year two, then goes to your normal rate. If rates drop during that time, you refinance and keep the lower payment.

2. Discount points — permanently lower your rate. One point (1% of the loan) typically cuts your rate by about 0.25%. And when the seller is paying for it? That's free savings for you.

This is the kind of strategy most buyers never hear about because most people don't have a lender walking them through the options.
