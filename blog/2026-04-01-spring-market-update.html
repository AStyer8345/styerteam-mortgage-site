Spring Market Update: What's Actually Happening in Austin Right Now

Real talk. Spring brings buyers back to Austin. We're in April now, and I'm seeing movement I didn't see in January or February. But it's not what most people expect.

Rates aren't dropping like they were supposed to. Inventory isn't exploding. But something shifted. Let me break down what's actually happening and what it means for you.

Rates Are Stuck in the Middle

If you were waiting for rates to hit 5% or lower this spring, I have some news. That's not what's happening.

Rates have been hovering between 6.5% and 7% depending on your loan type, credit, and down payment. They've bounced around, sure, but the big drop everyone was betting on? Didn't happen. The Fed's being cautious. Inflation is still a thing. Rates are going to stay elevated for longer than most people wanted.

Here's what matters: if you're a buyer right now, you're not competing with someone getting a crazy low rate. Everyone's in roughly the same boat. That changes the whole game compared to 2021 when rates were in the 2s.

Want to see what your rate would actually be? Use the rate calculator and plug in your real numbers. Don't guess.

Inventory Is Moving, But It's Not What You Think

Spring listings are up compared to winter. That part is normal. But here's the catch: homes in the $300K–$450K range are getting multiple offers within days. Anything nicer than that is sitting longer.

Translation: if you're looking at a well-priced home in a good school district, you need your financing locked in tight. Sellers can afford to wait you out. If you're building in a newer area or looking at something more niche, you have more time to think.

The Austin market is still supply-constrained compared to most cities. We're not seeing the massive inventory dumps from 2022. New construction is slower than it was, and people who locked in low rates years ago aren't selling.

What This Means for Buyers Right Now

If you've been on the fence about buying, spring changes the math a little.

First: your buying power is fixed. Rates aren't moving much, so your monthly payment on a $500K house isn't going to change dramatically. You either buy now or you wait six months for the same rate. That's not a reason to panic. It's just reality.

Second: competition is real again. Multiple offers are happening. That means your offer needs to be solid—good pre-qualification, proof of funds, clean inspection contingencies. If you're not pre-qualified yet, get that done this week. Seriously. It matters.

Third: timing matters more in spring. Sellers expect faster closings. Lenders have more volume. Appraisals take longer. If you need a mortgage, you need to start the process now, not in May when everything backs up.

What's Working in Your Favor

Here's the silver lining. Buyer demand is real, but it's not insane like 2021–2022. You can still negotiate. You can still ask for closing cost help. Appraisals are more stable. There's less weird cash-offer pressure.

Plus, if you're a current homeowner in Austin, your equity is solid. You have leverage in a trade-up situation. Banks are lending. Credit standards are normal, not stupid-loose. The whole thing feels more sane.

Real talk: if you want to buy in Austin, spring is when you do it. Not because rates are great. Not because it's exciting. But because you actually have options, the process works, and you're not fighting doomsday headlines.
