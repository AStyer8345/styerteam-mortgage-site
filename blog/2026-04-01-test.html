What You Need to Know About Rates Right Now

Mortgage rates shift constantly. Here's what's really happening with rates and what it means for your next move. Read the full breakdown.

A
Adam Styer
Loan Originator, NMLS# 513013 · April 1, 2026

What's Actually Happening With Mortgage Rates

Real talk: mortgage rates aren't moving in a straight line. They bounce around based on the bond market, inflation data, and what the Fed is doing. I know that sounds complicated, so let me break it down in a way that actually matters to you.

Right now, rates are sitting in a range that's honestly better than where they were a year ago, but higher than the historical lows we saw a few years back. That's just the reality. The question isn't whether rates are "good" or "bad" — it's whether they work for YOUR situation right now.

If you've been waiting for rates to drop another half point before you move, I get it. But here's what I see happen: people wait, rates don't cooperate, and then they're stuck waiting longer than they wanted. Meanwhile, their rent is going up, their financial situation is changing, and they miss the window.

How Rate Changes Actually Impact Your Payment

Let me show you something concrete. On a $400,000 loan in Austin:

  • At 6.0%, your payment is roughly $2,400/month
  • At 6.5%, you're at $2,532/month
  • At 5.5%, you're at $2,272/month

So yeah, every half point matters. But here's what I want you to really see: a $300/month difference is real money. But it's also not massive if you can actually afford the home itself. The bigger question is whether you can qualify, whether you have a down payment, and whether buying makes sense for your life right now.

Check out our mortgage calculators to run your own numbers. Plug in different rates and see what happens to your payment. Don't just believe what I'm telling you — see it yourself.

Should You Lock Your Rate or Wait?

This is the question I hear every single day. And I'm not going to tell you to lock or float because I genuinely don't know where rates go next week. Nobody does. Anyone who tells you they do is selling something.

What I CAN tell you: if you're serious about buying or refinancing in the next 30-45 days, locking your rate takes the stress out of the equation. You know exactly what your payment will be. You can make a decision based on the home and your life, not on rate anxiety.

If you're in no rush — and I mean genuinely in no rush — then floating makes sense. But "in no rush" usually means you're overthinking it. Most people are better off locking in a reasonable rate and moving forward.

The Austin Mortgage Market Right Now

Austin's market is interesting because home prices have cooled from the peak, but rates are still higher than they were. So you're looking at homes that might be reasonably priced by Austin standards, but your monthly payment is still significant because of where rates are.

That actually creates opportunity if you're actually ready to buy. Less competition. More inventory. Sellers more willing to negotiate. That's real.

If you're thinking about your move in Austin, getting pre-qualified is the first actual step. Not thinking about it. Not researching. Actually getting pre-qualified so you know what you can do and you have proof to show when you find the place you want.

What Happens If Rates Drop After You Lock?

Okay, worst case: you lock at 6.5% and next month rates are 6.0%. Here's what I tell people: you locked because you wanted certainty. You got it. You also got a home that you wanted to buy. That was the goal. Rates dropping after is just noise.

Could you refinance later? Maybe. Depends on rates, refinance costs, and whether it makes financial sense. But here's the thing — most people don't refinance anyway. They just wish they had. So lock, move forward, and stop second-guessing yourself.

Personal Corner

Been thinking a lot lately about how much energy we waste worrying about things we can't control. Rate movements, market swings, timing — none of that is up to us. What IS up to us is making a solid decision with the information we have right now, then committing to it. That's freedom. That's peace. Everything else is just noise.
