Buying a Home in Cedar Park: What You Need to Know

Cedar Park has earned a reputation as one of the best places to live in the Austin metro — and the data backs it up. Leander ISD, which serves Cedar Park, is consistently rated among the top school districts in Texas. The city offers easy access to the Apple campus in North Austin, major tech employers along the 183A toll corridor, and an increasingly vibrant restaurant and retail scene around 1890 Ranch and Bell Boulevard.

Homes in Cedar Park move quickly. The combination of excellent schools, safe neighborhoods, and reasonable commute times to Austin tech hubs makes Cedar Park perpetually popular with families relocating from California, the Pacific Northwest, and the Northeast. Sellers receive multiple offers on well-priced homes, especially in the $400,000 to $500,000 range.

Cedar Park Home Prices

Median prices in Cedar Park typically run $400,000 to $500,000, with newer master-planned communities and homes on larger lots commanding a premium. More established neighborhoods like Buttercup Creek and Twin Creeks offer slightly more value. The conforming loan limit of $832,750 covers virtually all Cedar Park purchases, meaning most buyers can use conventional financing.

Loan Programs for Cedar Park Buyers

Conventional (3–20% Down)

The most common choice for Cedar Park buyers. Strong credit scores, W-2 income, and stable employment make most Cedar Park buyers excellent conventional candidates. Rates are typically the lowest of any program for well-qualified borrowers.

FHA (3.5% Down)

Great for buyers with credit scores from 580 to 679, shorter employment history, or higher debt-to-income ratios. FHA is more flexible than conventional — a common entry point for first-time Cedar Park buyers.

VA (Zero Down)

Cedar Park's veteran community has excellent access to VA financing — zero down, no PMI, and the lowest rates of any program. If you or your spouse served in the military, this is almost always the best loan available.

Jumbo Loans

For Cedar Park homes priced above $832,750. Competitive portfolio lending available with as little as 10% down for well-qualified buyers. Rates are often closer to conventional than people expect.
