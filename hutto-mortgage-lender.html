Affordable new construction, proximity to the Samsung semiconductor plant in nearby Taylor, and easy access to the Dell and Apple corridors via US-79 and SH-130 have made Hutto a magnet for first-time buyers and young families looking for more house per dollar.

And at price points between $280K and $450K, many buyers qualify for down payment assistance programs they would not be eligible for in pricier Austin suburbs.

Home Prices in Hutto

Hutto home prices range from roughly $280,000 to $450,000, making it one of the most affordable markets in the greater Austin area. New construction dominates the inventory, with most builders pricing in the $300K–$400K sweet spot. Compared to Round Rock (10 minutes south) or Cedar Park (20 minutes west), Hutto offers $50,000 to $100,000 in savings on comparable homes — a significant advantage for first-time buyers stretching their budget.

Loan Options for Hutto Buyers

Conventional Loans

The most popular option for Hutto buyers with good credit. As little as 3% down with competitive rates. Well within the 2026 conforming limit of $832,750, so every Hutto home qualifies for conventional financing with the best available pricing.

FHA Loans

Ideal for first-time buyers and those with credit scores as low as 580. Just 3.5% down and more flexible debt-to-income ratios than conventional loans. Hutto's price points make FHA an excellent fit — your monthly payment stays affordable even with MIP factored in.

VA Loans

Zero down payment, no PMI, and the lowest rates available for eligible veterans and active duty military. Hutto's growing veteran population and proximity to military-connected employers make VA financing a strong option in this market.

Down Payment Assistance

Texas DPA programs through TSAHC and TDHCA offer grants and forgivable second loans covering your down payment and closing costs. Hutto's affordable price points mean more buyers qualify for these programs. Adam will check your eligibility and stack DPA with the right loan to minimize your out-of-pocket costs.

Hutto Mortgage FAQ

Hutto home prices typically range from $280,000 to $450,000. New construction in communities like Star Ranch, Riverwalk, and Emory Crossing falls in the $300K–$400K range. Resale homes in established neighborhoods can be found starting in the high $200s. Hutto consistently ranks as one of the most affordable new-construction markets in the Austin metro.

Buying new construction in Hutto works like a standard home purchase — you get pre-approved, sign a contract with the builder, and close when the home is complete. Builders often push their preferred lender, but you are not required to use them. Adam Styer frequently beats builder-lender rates and fees, and using an independent broker gives you someone working for you — not for the builder.

Hutto buyers have access to FHA loans (3.5% down, 580+ credit score), conventional loans with 3% down, and Texas down payment assistance programs through TSAHC and TDHCA. These DPA programs offer grants and forgivable second loans that cover your down payment and part of your closing costs. Hutto's affordable price points mean more buyers meet the income limits for these programs.

Hutto offers significantly lower home prices — often $50,000 to $100,000 less than comparable homes in Round Rock. The tradeoff is a slightly longer commute, about 10 minutes farther from central Austin. Hutto ISD is growing rapidly and investing in new schools. For buyers who prioritize a newer home, more space, and lower monthly payments, Hutto is hard to beat.

Adam Styer's average closing timeline is 21 days for resale homes. New construction closings depend on the builder's completion schedule — typically 30 to 60 days from when the home is ready. Adam stays ahead of the builder's timeline so financing is never the reason for a delay.
