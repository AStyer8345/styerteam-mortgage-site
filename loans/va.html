What Is a VA Loan?

A VA loan is a mortgage guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, available exclusively to active-duty service members, veterans, and eligible surviving spouses. VA loans are widely considered the best mortgage program in the country, offering benefits that no other loan type can match: zero down payment, no private mortgage insurance, no prepayment penalties, and consistently lower interest rates than conventional or FHA financing.

Austin is home to a growing military-connected community, with proximity to Fort Cavazos (formerly Fort Hood) and numerous veteran-owned businesses. Whether you are transitioning out of the military, relocating to Austin for a new career, or an established veteran looking to upgrade, a VA loan gives you maximum buying power with minimum out-of-pocket costs.

VA Loan Advantages at a Glance

  • Zero down payment — 100% financing on any home up to the appraised value
  • No PMI — saves $150-400+/month compared to conventional loans with less than 20% down
  • Lowest rates — VA rates are typically 0.25-0.50% lower than conventional rates
  • No prepayment penalty — pay off or refinance your loan anytime
  • Flexible credit — no minimum credit score required by the VA (lenders set their own, typically 580-620)
  • Reusable benefit — use your VA loan benefit multiple times throughout your life
  • Assumable — a future buyer can take over your VA loan at your locked-in rate

Who Is Eligible for a VA Loan?

Active Duty Service Members

Currently serving with at least 90 consecutive days of active duty. All branches of the U.S. military are eligible including Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Coast Guard, and Space Force.

Veterans

Former service members who served the minimum active duty requirement and received anything other than a dishonorable discharge. Service during wartime requires 90 days; peacetime requires 181 days.

National Guard & Reserves

Members with 6 or more years of service in the Guard or Reserves, or 90 days of active duty under Title 10 orders. Eligibility has expanded significantly in recent years.

Surviving Spouses

Un-remarried surviving spouses of veterans who died in the line of duty, from a service-connected disability, or while on active duty. These spouses are also exempt from the VA funding fee.

VA Funding Fee Explained

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge that helps sustain the VA loan program. It replaces what would otherwise be monthly mortgage insurance. The fee varies based on your service type, down payment, and whether this is your first VA loan use:

ScenarioFirst UseSubsequent Use
0% down2.15%3.3%
5-9% down1.5%1.5%
10%+ down1.25%1.25%

Exempt from funding fee: Veterans with service-connected disabilities, Purple Heart recipients on active duty, and surviving spouses. The fee can be financed into the loan amount.
