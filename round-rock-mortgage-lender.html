What Closing Costs Should Round Rock Buyers Expect?

At Round Rock's $388,000 median price, buyer closing costs typically run $8,000–$12,000 (roughly 2–3% of the purchase price). Real-number breakdown:

  • Lender's title insurance: ~$1,741 (Texas DOI-regulated rate on a $390K purchase)
  • Escrow/settlement fee: ~$600
  • Recording fees: ~$125 (Williamson County)
  • Property tax proration: At the ~1.68% combined rate, a $390,000 home generates ~$6,552/year in taxes. Closing mid-year? The seller credits you ~$3,276 for the 6 months of accrued taxes they owe. Source: Texas Property Calculator
  • Prepaids (first year homeowner's insurance + initial escrow deposit): ~$3,000–$4,000

Seller concessions toward closing costs are realistic in Round Rock's current market. Ask me what's negotiable at your price point before you make an offer.

What Loan Programs Are Available for Round Rock TX Homebuyers?

Conventional Loans

The most popular choice for Round Rock buyers with solid credit and stable income. As little as 3% down, competitive rates, and fast closings. The 2026 conforming limit is $832,750 — covering most Round Rock homes.

FHA Loans

Perfect for first-time buyers or those rebuilding credit. 3.5% down with a 580+ credit score. Higher debt-to-income allowances make this the go-to option when income is good but credit history is short.

VA Loans

Zero down payment, no PMI, and the lowest rates available for eligible veterans and active duty military. Round Rock's proximity to Fort Cavazos makes VA financing a common and excellent option here.

Down Payment Assistance

Texas programs like TSAHC and TDHCA offer grants and forgivable second loans to eligible buyers. Income limits apply, but many Round Rock buyers qualify. Ask Adam which DPA programs are available to you.
