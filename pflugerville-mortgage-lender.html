What Closing Costs Should Pflugerville Buyers Expect?

At Pflugerville's $355,000 median, buyer closing costs typically run $8,000–$11,000 (roughly 2.2–3.1% of the purchase price). Real-number breakdown:

  • Lender's title insurance: ~$1,600 (Texas DOI-promulgated rate on a $355K purchase)
  • Escrow/settlement fee: ~$600
  • Recording fees: ~$150 (Travis County — $25 per instrument first page, $4 per additional page, per Travis County)
  • Property tax proration: Pflugerville Travis County addresses carry a combined rate (City of Pflugerville + Travis County + PISD) of approximately 2.0–2.1%, per City of Pflugerville tax rate page and PISD's adopted $1.1069/$100 rate. At 2.0% on a $355K home, that's ~$7,100/year. Closing mid-year? The seller credits you roughly $3,550 for 6 months of accrued taxes they owe.
  • Prepaids (first year homeowner's insurance + initial escrow deposit): ~$3,000–$3,500

Seller concessions toward closing costs are negotiable in Pflugerville's current buyer-friendly market. Ask me what's realistic at your price point before you make an offer.

What Loan Programs Are Available for Pflugerville TX Homebuyers?

Conventional Loans

3–20% down, 620+ credit score, no upfront mortgage insurance. Best for buyers with solid credit who want to keep monthly costs competitive. Pflugerville's median price range works well within conventional loan limits.

FHA Loans

3.5% down with credit scores as low as 580. More flexible DTI ratios. Pflugerville's price range fits comfortably within FHA loan limits. Popular with first-time buyers who prioritize low down payment over monthly payment optimization.

VA Loans

Zero down for eligible veterans, active duty, and surviving spouses. No monthly PMI. A significant number of Pflugerville residents have military background — this is consistently the best loan program available to eligible buyers.

Investment Property Loans

Pflugerville's strong rental demand from the growing workforce makes it an interesting market for investment property. I work with lenders offering competitive investment property financing — traditional 20–25% down options as well as DSCR loans that qualify on rental income rather than personal income.
