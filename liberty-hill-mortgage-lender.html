Why Should Liberty Hill Buyers Use an Independent Mortgage Broker?

Liberty Hill has gone from a quiet Hill Country town along SH-29 to one of the hottest growth corridors in the Austin metro. The explosion of master-planned communities — Santa Rita Ranch, Northgate Ranch, Briggs Ranch, Rio Ancho — has brought thousands of new homes and families to an area that still feels like small-town Texas. Liberty Hill ISD, a tight-knit downtown, and Hill Country views keep drawing buyers who want space and community without giving up access to Austin, just 35 minutes south.

Most of the growth here is spillover from Cedar Park and Leander, where prices have pushed many families farther north. Liberty Hill offers comparable new construction at lower price points and larger lots. For buyers who work remotely or commute a few days a week, the value equation is compelling.

Home Prices in Liberty Hill

The Liberty Hill median sale price was $485,000 in February 2026 — down 6.3% from $517,500 in February 2025 — across 53 closed homes (Neuhaus Realty Group, Unlock MLS data). New construction in master-planned communities falls in the $375K–$550K range. Custom builds on acreage and larger properties can exceed $600K. All Liberty Hill homes fall well within the 2026 conforming loan limit of $832,750, keeping conventional financing rates competitive — for purchases above the limit, jumbo financing applies.

Liberty Hill at a glance: Entirely within Liberty Hill ISD — the district has built out rapidly to keep pace with growth. Commute times: ~35 min to the Domain and North Austin tech corridor via SH-183A Toll/US-183 (off-peak), ~45 min to downtown Austin via US-183, ~20 min to Cedar Park and Leander, ~25 min to Georgetown via Ronald Reagan Blvd. Combined property tax rates inside city limits run roughly 2.0%–2.4% — meaningfully lower than Travis County's 2.2%–2.7%, which translates to real monthly savings for buyers relocating from Austin proper.

Liberty Hill Neighborhoods & Zip Codes

Liberty Hill Schools

Liberty Hill ISD covers the entire city. Liberty Hill High School currently rates 7/10 on GreatSchools with a 98% graduation rate (vs. the 90% Texas state average). Voters approved Proposition A in November 2025, raising the M&O tax rate to $0.7389 per $100 to fund teacher salaries and program continuity (Community Impact, Nov 2025). Combined with the I&S rate of $0.50, the LHISD school rate is $1.2389 per $100. The district is also expanding with a $23M upgrade to Liberty Hill High School and a planned new Legacy Ranch High School.

Major Employers & Local Economy

Liberty Hill ISD is the largest employer with approximately 600 staff (Liberty Hill EDC). Two major retail projects are under construction along the US-183/SH-29 corridor: an estimated $75M Costco Wholesale and a $22M Target. The Platform 183 x 29 mixed-use project is set to deliver more than 1 million square feet of industrial space (Liberty Hill Digest, Aug 2025). Beyond city limits, the Samsung Taylor fab, Tesla Gigafactory, Apple, and Dell are within commuting distance — Liberty Hill is ~42 miles from Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.

Closing Cost Example

On a $485K Liberty Hill home with 5% down (loan amount ~$460,750), expect roughly $10,500 to $13,000 in closing costs (excluding the down payment itself). Typical breakdown: title insurance at the Texas-promulgated rate (~$2,500), lender origination/processing/underwriting (~$1,400), appraisal (~$650), survey (~$500), recording/courier (~$200), and prepaids — 1 year homeowners insurance (~$2,800), 3 months tax escrow (~$2,400), and pro-rated property tax. Combined city + LHISD + Williamson County rates run roughly 2.0%–2.4%, so annual property taxes on a $485K home land between $9,700 and $11,600. What Loan Options Are Available for Liberty Hill TX Homebuyers?

Conventional Loans

The dominant choice for Liberty Hill buyers. As little as 3% down with competitive rates through Adam's wholesale lender network. Every Liberty Hill home falls under the 2026 conforming limit of $832,750, so you get the best available conventional pricing across the board.

New Construction Loans

Building a custom home on Hill Country acreage? A one-time close construction loan combines the lot purchase, construction, and permanent mortgage into one loan with one closing. Adam coordinates with your builder on draw schedules and keeps the project on track from permit to move-in.

VA Loans

Jumbo Loans
